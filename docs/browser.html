You Are Being Watched!

Digital fingerprinting is the process where a remote site or service covers little bits of information about the user's machine and puts those pieces together to form a unique picture or fingerprint of the user's device. Cookies are things that a website stores on your computer in order to keep track of your activities information that a website can use in order to make your future visits faster and more convenient like login information, language settings, etc. But most of the information is used that they can better serve you with personalised ads and products youâ€™re more likely to buy the next time you visit. The super information can also be stored on the website servers so you only get an ID that links to it now if you delete that cookie/ID the link is gone and they have no way of knowing who you are. This is where fingerprinting comes into play as it creates a link between you and the information they have on you that is borderline impossible to break. When you click on the link in order to visit the website your web browser send a request to the website server along with information which is necessary for you to actually receive the requested content typically that would include things like your IP address and the particular browser you are using.
